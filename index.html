Toolkit

Ancestry & class traits, in brief settled
  • Blue dara: Medium size, 30-ft speed, three skill proficiencies of choice, and paper talismans (Sun, Earth, or Moon; 1/long rest) — plus the blue dara’s daily Knowledge Talisman (a d6 boost to an ability check). The Earth Talisman — keeping someone on their feet — is deeply on-brand.
  • Sacred Revelation: he can read the thumbprint glyphs left across the island by other dara — the trail his self-discovery may follow.
  • Languages: Common and Archaic First Age Languages — the latter inherited through his parting glyph. He can read the old world’s script and does not know why. His predecessor could.
  • Belly Brewer: he brews concoctions in his own gut — combat, utility, or whimsy — activated when he rages. The most chaotic cauldron on the island, carried under his apron. Side Effects (3rd level): the countless magical ingredients he’s ingested have left him with the Prestidigitation cantrip (warm, flavor, and clean — kitchen magic) and Thaumaturgy (booming voice, trembling ground).
  • Barbarian 2–3: Reckless Attack (advantage on Str attacks this turn; attacks against him gain advantage too — the lid comes off) and Danger Sense (advantage on Dex saves vs. effects he can see).

She gave Obu pointers and a little teaching in potion craft, and let him study — though never out of her “sight” — her volume of Granny Prumple’s Almanac, taking notes on the basics. The Chef

Who he is settled

A nurturer first and always. Obu believes in fair play and kindness, and there is a cup of tea or a snack in his hands for anyone who needs one. He is a full seven feet of stout, hairless, blue-skinned dara with a single enormous eye that misses nothing — least of all an empty stomach or an unhappy face.

Inspirations: Dr. Chef (The Long Way to a Small, Angry Planet), Cadwallader (Lightfall), and Ringo (Blue Eye Samurai) — the gentle caretaker whose kindness is a practice, not a passivity.

The Springs & the Stand

Life before the road settled

Obu grew up among the hot springs of Onaka. He learned to cook by soaking plants and herbs in the mineral spring water and sharing the results with villagers and travelers — patience cooking, where the springs do the work and Obu’s genius is knowing which pool, which herbs, and how long.

The belly brewing came the same unhurried way: munching on snacks while soaking in the hot springs, Obu discovered that certain morsels, eaten together in that mineral warmth, did their brewing inside him. He became a simmering pool himself — one more vessel among Onaka’s waters.

In time he opened his own food stand in town. Nobody officially named it; the village just started saying “meet you at Obu’s,” and it stuck. It became very popular. He didn’t close it when he left — he took it with him. Wherever the pot goes down: “Obu’s is open.”

The Kettle

The temper, and why he left settled

Obu has a hot temper. When he sees injustice — a bully, someone taking advantage of another — the kettle boils over and he slips into his rage. He is deeply embarrassed by this. Dara are supposed to be level-headed, observant, neutral; every flare of his temper feels like failing at being dara. He doesn’t know whether the anger came with his parting glyph, or whether something is simply wrong with him.

Folks in Onaka had seen him lose his temper a little, here and there. But not long ago he lost it in front of a crowd. The village reassured him it was okay — and he left anyway, because the shame was internal. He set out to travel, clear his head, and maybe learn who he is. Onaka is still warm, still fond of him, still waiting. He just can’t go back yet.

Two Errands (He Thinks)

Why he’s on the road settled
  • The Chicken Chaser potion. More than a year ago, a pilgrim passing through Onaka told Obu of a very rare potion that summons a hundred chickens. Obu became obsessed: imagine summoning flocks for towns and villages struggling to grow food! He seeks the recipe and its rare ingredients across Obojima — the errand that first points him toward Fiamut.
  • The other errand. Figure himself out. Learn where the temper comes from — the dara he inherited from, or himself — and what to do about it.

He believes these are two separate errands. They are not.

What Obu doesn’t know

The Chicken Chaser is as much weapon as wonder — the summoned flock is devastating to everything caught in it. The gentle thing he’s chasing has teeth it doesn’t know about. So does he.

One Turn of Combat

Obu’s menu of options each round — one of each colored box per turn

Move

Up to 30 ft · splittable

Walk up to 30 feet, split freely before/after/between anything else on this card. Difficult terrain costs double.

Action

Pick one
  • Attack — one swing of the frying pan: d20 + 5 to hit; damage 1d6 + 3 one-handed or 1d8 + 3 two-handed (+2 more while raging). He can attack recklessly: advantage on Str attacks this turn, but attacks against him gain advantage until his next turn. Instead of damaging, an attack can be a grapple or shove (Athletics contest — his +5 shines here).
  • Standard actions — Dash (double move), Disengage (no opportunity attacks), Dodge (attacks vs. him at disadvantage), Help an ally, Hide, Search, or Use an Object (pour a potion down an ally’s throat, upend the stew pot).
  • Cast a cantrip — Prestidigitation (clean, warm, flavor, snuff or light small flames, minor sensory effects) or Thaumaturgy (booming voice, trembling ground, doors slam, flames flicker). Not while raging — barbarians can’t cast during a rage; the kitchen magic only works when the kettle isn’t boiling.
  • Create a talisman — one of Sun, Earth, or Moon (1/long rest), plus his blue-dara Knowledge Talisman (its own 1/long rest). Each is a paper charm he hands to someone (or keeps); it lasts 1 hour or until it activates.
    Pool 1 — pick one of these per long rest:
    • Sun — the holder can Dash or Disengage as a bonus action. Lasts 1 hour or until activated.
    • Earth — if the holder would drop to 0 HP (and isn’t killed outright), they drop to 1 HP instead; activates by itself. Lasts 1 hour or until activated.
    • Moon — placed on a weapon (an action); its next hit deals extra radiant damage equal to Obu’s level (3). Lasts 1 hour or until activated.
    Pool 2 — separate daily use (blue dara gift):
    • Knowledge — the holder adds 1d6 to any ability check, rollable before or after seeing the d20. Not attack rolls or saves. Lasts 1 hour or until activated — so make it when the moment is near, not at breakfast.

Bonus Action

Pick one
  • Enter Rage (3/long rest) — the kettle boils over: +2 melee damage, resistance to bludgeoning/piercing/slashing, advantage on Str checks and saves. Choosing a belly concoction (combat, utility, or whimsy) happens as part of raging.
    Only while raging — each unlocked by the concoction chosen:
    • Stretch arms (Combat) — rubber-body reach +5 ft for the turn; first hit deals +1d6.
    • Swallow & heal (Utility) — gulp down an object, spend one of his 3 Hit Dice, regain 1d12 + 3 HP (2 uses/long rest).
    • Whimsy effects (Whimsy) — at the start of each turn: a short teleport, a self-zap that boosts his next ability check, or Help as a bonus action.

    Entering the rage takes this turn’s bonus action — the options above start on his next turn.

  • Raise the pot lid — while wielding the frying pan: +1 AC until his next turn (stacking on the lid’s passive +1, for AC 16).
  • Pot lid bash? — the pan’s Special allows a bonus-action pot lid attack (1d4) when attacking with a finesse or light weapon; whether the pan itself qualifies is an ask-the-DM.
  • Activate a Sun Talisman he’s holding — Dash or Disengage as a bonus action.

Reaction

One per round · fires on a trigger, usually on someone else’s turn · recharges at the start of his turn
  • Opportunity attack when an enemy leaves his reach — one free pan swing.
  • Belly deflect (raging, Combat concoction) — when hit by a ranged attack, reduce the damage by 1d12 + 6 (1d12 + Con + barbarian level).

Free Interaction

Once per turn · no cost

Interact with one object (draw the pan, snatch a snack off his belt) and speak briefly. “Obu’s is open” costs nothing. A second object interaction in the same turn requires the Use an Object action.

Talismans in someone’s possession activate on their turn, not Obu’s — the Knowledge Talisman’s d6 rides along with an ability check, and the Earth Talisman fires by itself when its holder would drop to 0 HP.
